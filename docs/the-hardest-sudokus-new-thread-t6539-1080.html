denis_berthier wrote:
mith wrote:There is then the question of what to do about puzzles which are the same after *basics* but not after just singles. I don’t have any stats on how frequently this is the case among the hard puzzles, but when we’re talking about 11.x puzzles a cutoff at basics makes as much sense as a cutoff at singles to me.

Statistically, it will not make much difference. But sorry, definitely no; "basics" doesn't make any sense. "Basics" are not even closed under rule isomorphisms (they include Pairs but not X-Wings).


FWIW, basics for me would include fish (since fish are just subsets in another dimension). Maybe we need a better term for this, since basics probably means something different to most people.

You're correct that applying non-singles subsets would produce a sukaku, but I don't think this ultimately matters. The important thing is what digits can be determined by applying these rules - if the resulting digit grids are isomorphic, they sukaku obtained by continuing eliminations will also be isomorphic; and if the digit grids aren't isomorphic, no amount of subset/whip eliminations will make the sukaku isomorphic if no new digits are placed.

All that said, for now I will just apply singles for the comparison.

At the moment, most of my choices in the database are to facilitate generating new puzzles efficiently. The list presented above is formatted for readability moreso than as a proposed long-term format for the project; the actual database is in SQLite, and it's simple enough to adjust the output.

Regarding the points raised by Denis:

1. My database has two separate fields, 'db_puzzle' and 'minlex_puzzle'. The former is the morph of the puzzle that was in ph_2010 or posted on the forums; the later is what I use as a canonical form (not true minlex, but consistent, using gsf's minlex form). I don't currently have a tool set up for generating the maxlex canonical form, but may add that in the future. (For puzzles I have generated, 'db_puzzle' is usually empty, so when posted here they are in 'minlex_puzzle' form. Occasionally I will morph a puzzle into a nicer form, as with Loki, at which point I will add this form to the database in 'db_puzzle'.)

2. I think long-term I will generate the expanded form for all the puzzles (or at least all the puzzles with sufficiently high rating) and store this form in a separate database (along with adding the expanded form or an index to it in a new column in the minlex database). This is going to take a while, however - currently there are over 32 million puzzles in my local database (not all would be included in an update, as most are only rated by skfr_ER).

3. Already resolved.

4. I agree on the puzzle part - puzzlesXXc is just the name of the database/table (there is a separate database per count, and the rowid used as a unique identifier is therefore specific to the database for that count). I do think the count is worth keeping, even if it is available in a separate field - not that just a ton of people are going to be using this thing, but it's easy to imagine someone referring to a puzzle only by the 'name' field and not specifying a count.

5. Separate fields in the database, I just combined them for display purposes here. (Likewise for the ratings; I have separate fields for ER, EP, ED, as well as skfr_ER etc.)

6. A csv is easily imported into excel; the problem with publishing as an excel sheet is the size. It would be fine for only 11.6+ puzzles, say, but even an 11.0+ cutoff (counting skfr_ER for puzzles not yet rated by SER) is closing in on 4 million entries at this point.

7. I have a field that is used for this purpose - basically a time stamp for when a large update is posted. For a full updated, I will likely populate this field as ph2010 for all puzzles in that database, and as ph22XX for puzzles in the new update (it would be possible to go back to older ph updates and give a more complete history, as well). Additionally, I intend to carry over the internal sequence number champagne used.

8. As mentioned in 2, likely I will just create a separate database for this. Fields would included 'minlex_expanded', ER/EP/ED, # of clues, # of minimals, max/min # clues in minimals... other fields which might be useful on my end but less so for a published update would be max/min ER for minimals, whether all minimals are in the minimal databases, things like that. Would need to figure out how best to credit 'creator' and how to name them, as well - could do this based on the oldest minimal in the database, perhaps, though that won't always be clear for puzzles after ph2010.

9. This would be reasonable to calculate alongside generating the expanded forms.
